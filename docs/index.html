The arc

This is the sequel.

AI-Trainer-MAX teaches a Windows user with no prior experience how to install, run, and own a local AI — all the way through to building a digital legacy their family can still run after they're gone.

OBLIVION is what comes after that.

It is the research-math layer MAX deliberately left out. The part where the training wheels come off and you stop being a user of models and start being someone who shapes them.

It is not for beginners. It is not hand-holding.
It assumes you finished MAX and want more.

Planned arc

What OBLIVION will cover.

No release date yet. This is the terrain. Each item maps to a module or cluster of modules once the curriculum is finalized.

01
Fine-tuning on your own hardware
The actual mechanics. Not hand-waving. Not someone else's Colab.
02
LoRA / QLoRA on consumer GPUs
8GB, 12GB, 24GB — what's possible at each tier and the honest trade-offs.
03
Quantization trade-offs
GGUF, Q4 vs Q5 vs Q8, when each wins, when they break.
04
Custom Modelfiles at depth
System prompts as architecture, not cosmetics. Stop decorating. Start designing.
05
Training-data curation
How to build the dataset that matters. Quality over quantity. Signal over noise.
06
Synthetic data generation pipelines
Making your own training data, ethically, at scale, without a cloud bill.
07
Eval harnesses
Measuring "better" instead of feeling it. Writing tests your model has to pass.
08
Prompt-to-weights reasoning
What a gradient actually does. Why your loss curve is lying to you.
09
Embedding-space geometry
How retrieval really works under the hood. Why your RAG gets confused.
10
Running your fine-tune in production
The part nobody documents. Serving, updating, rolling back when it breaks.
Gate

Prerequisites.

This one has a door. These are the things you need to walk through it.

  • Completed all 5 phases of AI-Trainer-MAX
  • A machine with more than 16GB of RAM — and preferably a GPU
  • Comfort at the command line. Not fluency. Comfort.
  • Patience. Runs will fail. That is part of the course.
If you're here without having finished MAX
Finish MAX first. OBLIVION will still be here.
